Q2. Why is Kelvin used in science?

Kelvin begins at absolute zero and establishes an accurate scale for thermodynamic temperature, which is essential in medical situations. The Kelvin scale never goes negative, which makes it ideal for scientific equations involving pressure, volume, and energy.
