Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. How do I convert Celsius to Kelvin?

To convert Celsius to Kelvin, we can simply add 273.15 to the Celsius temperature as K = °C + 273.15. This is because the Kelvin scale starts at absolute zero, which is -273.15°C. For example, 25°C is equal to 298.15 K. This formula is useful in scientific calculations where absolute temperature is required, particularly in physics and chemistry experiments.

Q2. What’s 0 Celsius in Kelvin?

0 degrees Celsius equals 273.15 Kelvin. This is calculated using the formula K = °C + 273.15. Since 0°C is the freezing point of water, in Kelvin it becomes 273.15 K. Kelvin is often used in scientific contexts because it begins at absolute zero, the lowest possible temperature where molecular motion stops.

Q3. Why is Kelvin used in science?

Kelvin is used in science because it starts at absolute zero, the point where all molecular motion stops. This makes it ideal for measuring thermal energy. Unlike Celsius or Fahrenheit, Kelvin has no negative values, simplifying scientific calculations in physics, chemistry, and thermodynamics. It’s also the SI unit for temperature in the International System of Units.

Q4. What is 25 Celsius in Kelvin?

25 degrees Celsius equals 298.15 Kelvin. To convert, use the formula K = °C + 273.15. So, 25 + 273.15 = 298.15 K. This temperature is commonly used in science, especially in chemistry and physics, as it represents standard room temperature in absolute terms for precise measurements and calculations.
